# Lens: Register & Tone

Apply when the input's formality, tone, or connotation matters — i.e. almost always for real communication. Helps the user sound appropriate, not just correct.

## What to produce
**Register & Tone（语域与语气）**
- **Level**: casual / neutral / professional / formal / academic.
- **Connotation & attitude**: emotional color, politeness, directness, any implied stance.
- **Fit**: in which contexts this lands well, and where it would feel off.

Explain in the anchor language where it aids understanding.

## Principles
- Distinguish *wrong* from *mismatched*: a sentence can be grammatical yet wrong for the setting.
- Flag risky tone explicitly (too blunt, too casual for a superior, unintentionally cold).
- Tie to the user's `profile.md` preferred register when known.

## Example
"I need this by Friday." → Level: neutral-direct. Connotation: clear but can read as curt to a senior colleague. Fit: peers/reports fine; for a manager prefer "Could you get this to me by Friday?" 中文：语气直接、略显命令式，对上级宜软化。
